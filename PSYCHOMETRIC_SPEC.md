## Part 1: Emotion Regulation Questionnaire (ERQ)
**Source:** Gross, J.J., & John, O.P. (2003). Individual differences in two emotion regulation processes: Implications for affect, relationships, and well-being. [span_4](start_span)Journal of Personality and Social Psychology, 85, 348-362[span_4](end_span).

### Instructions
[span_5](start_span)We would like to ask you some questions about your emotional life, in particular, how you control (that is, regulate and manage) your emotions[span_5](end_span). [span_6](start_span)The questions below involve two distinct aspects of your emotional life[span_6](end_span). [span_7](start_span)One is your emotional experience, or what you feel like inside[span_7](end_span). [span_8](start_span)The other is your emotional expression, or how you show your emotions in the way you talk, gesture, or behave[span_8](end_span). [span_9](start_span)Although some of the following questions may seem similar to one another, they differ in important ways[span_9](end_span). [span_10](start_span)For each item, please answer using the following scale[span_10](end_span):

### Items
* 1. [span_16](start_span)When I want to feel more positive emotion (such as joy or amusement), I change what I'm thinking about.[span_16](end_span)
* 2. [span_17](start_span)I keep my emotions to myself.[span_17](end_span)
* 3. [span_18](start_span)When I want to feel less negative emotion (such as sadness or anger), I change what I'm thinking about.[span_18](end_span)
* 4. [span_19](start_span)When I am feeling positive emotions, I am careful not to express them.[span_19](end_span)
